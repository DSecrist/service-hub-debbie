During cold weather months, your battery could lose over 30% of its power. Here are ways you can ensure that your BMW’s battery performs optimally during winter months:
    Routine Checks
    BMW Certified Technicians use special battery-testing equipment to run a variety of tests to find out how your battery will fare through winter. They let you know of any potential problems that could affect your battery and fix them for you.

    Cleanings
    Battery terminals should be cleaned out periodically, especially at the start of the winter season. Remember, any build-up over the summer can discharge the battery even when it isn’t running.

    Get A Replacement
    If the car’s battery is more than three years old, it is a good idea to have a BMW Certified Technician inspect its health. They will test the battery’s charge and let you know if you need to get a new one.

    Keep It Warm
    During cold weather months, park your car in a sheltered spot so the battery does not freeze.
Colder temperatures can make it difficult for the battery to start your car. If you are concerned about your BMW’s battery, our experts can perform an inspection and let you know about its health. Make sure you bring in your BMW before winter, so you aren’t stuck with a car that won’t start.
