When you drive off the dealer lot, often you notice the new car smell, the crisp interior and the smooth ride. As time goes on, using the car for your daily commute or long distance trips, your ride is not as it used to be, why is that? Have our certified technicians inspect your vehicle’s tires and alignment, plenty of things can cause your car’s wheels to become misaligned from heavy impacts when driving.
    Hitting Potholes
    Often out of our control, the heavy wear and tear that our roads experience affect our vehicles as well. Potholes are incredibly common and only increase their impact on our vehicles if we continuously have to drive over them. A small unsuspecting pothole can have major consequences for your BMW’s suspension system.

    Scraping or Bumping Curbs
    Much like potholes, scraping curbs can have serious effects on your vehicle’s suspension. Unassuming curbs can wreak havoc on your tires and the other components in your suspension systems.

    Minor Accidents
    A fender-bender can cause components of you suspension to bend or shift out of place. Coupled with normal wear and tear of your daily driving habits your car’s tires can come out of alignment, with misalignment comes that new, less than comfortable ride.
