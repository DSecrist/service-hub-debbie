How often should I replace my wiper blades?
Wiper blades should be replaced every 6 months. You may want to replace them earlier if you live in particular harsh environment. The best way to identify worn wiper blades is to notice any changes in windshield visibility while driving.

Common Wiper Problems
    Streaking
    Occurs when the rubber squeegee hardens and dries from age. Streaking can also occur if foreign substances like tree sap or pollen have collected on the glass or blade.

    Skipping
    is caused by a wiper blade developing a curvature from lack of use.

    Squeaking
    Can indicate the blade is beginning to dry out or breakdown. When properly aligned a wiper blade should make full contact with the windshield and follow a smooth path.

    Splitting
    Occurs when the blade it too dry and hard. Extended periods in the sun causes the rubber squeegee to breakdown and separate from the frame.
